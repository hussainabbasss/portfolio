# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and senior engineers evaluating a web developer. They arrive from a resume link or a GitHub profile, skim in under 60 seconds, and decide whether to reach out. Secondary: peers and clients checking credibility. Context: desktop at work, phone in transit — both must land.

## Product Purpose

A single-page personal portfolio whose job is to convert a visit into contact. It must prove craft *by being* the craft: the page itself is the strongest project in the list. Success = the visitor remembers this page specifically among dozens of candidate portfolios, and clicks the email or a project link.

## Brand Personality

Precise, senior, machined. Physical-object words: milled aluminum, engraved brass plate, an engineer's bound dossier. Calm authority — terse copy, metrics do the talking, zero hype adjectives. The one permitted flourish is typographic scale; everything else earns its place.

## Anti-references

- The generic template portfolio: centered hero + circular avatar, "Hi, I'm ___" over a gradient blob, stacked identical Bootstrap-ish cards.
- The saturated 2026 editorial-typographic fingerprint: italic display serif + tiny tracked mono eyebrows above every section + ruled three-column monochrome. We borrow editorial *scale*, not that grammar.
- Terminal cosplay: fake shells, blinking cursors, `~/whoami` gimmicks.
- Hero-metric SaaS template: big number / small label / gradient accent rows.

## Design Principles

1. **The page is the portfolio.** Every detail (perf, a11y, motion) is a work sample. Nothing ships that a senior reviewer would flag.
2. **Specs over adjectives.** Numbers, stacks, and impact lines carry the persuasion; copy stays terse.
3. **One identity, fully committed.** Engineering-dossier: editorial display type on a machined near-black surface, amber-gold as the single brand color, ending in a full amber drench. No element outside the system.
4. **Fast is a feature.** No heavy libraries, system-grade performance, Lighthouse 95+.
5. **Accessible by construction.** WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-first nav, reduced-motion parity — not a retrofit.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1, large display ≥3:1. Full keyboard operability with visible focus. `prefers-reduced-motion` gets crossfade/static equivalents for every animation, including the hero canvas. Semantic landmarks and labeled icon links.
